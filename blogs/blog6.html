Blog - Week 6

UX and UX of Websites

By Kellen Strydom

Temu.com

Temu, a newcomer in South Africa's online retail landscape, aggressively advertises its product range at cheap competitive prices, aiming to carve out a foothold in the market. While its homepage design adheres to a consistent colour scheme reflective of its brand identity, an excessive use of orange takes away from the effectiveness in highlighting crucial information. Temu makes its interfaces familiar, facilitating a seamless user experience with navigation elements like popular platforms like Takealot. The platform's grid layout of products leverages the user's inclination for continuous scrolling, potentially fostering addictive browsing behaviour. Although Temu excels in streamlining the checkout process with a three-click system, its perpetual sales promotions and implementation of limited-time offers with countdown timers exploit psychological triggers such as the fear of missing out and perceived scarcity to spur impulsive purchases. Despite its user-friendly interface, Temu's reliance on such tactics raises ethical concerns regarding user manipulation and excessive consumerism.

Takealot.co.za

Takealot's website embodies a clean and simple design, featuring a user-friendly interface that prioritizes ease of navigation. The inclusion of clear directional symbols ensures seamless exploration, guiding users effortlessly through the platform. With an intuitive layout, Takealot optimizes the browsing experience, making it straightforward for users to locate desired products and categories. Overall, Takealot's UI and UX design strike a balance between simplicity and functionality, fostering a pleasant and efficient online shopping journey for all South Africans.

Amazon.com

Amazon.com boasts a clean and organized layout, facilitating seamless navigation across its diverse product categories. The inclusion of a convenient one-click purchasing option enhances the checkout process for returning customers, optimizing efficiency. However, the website's limited customization options for the user interface may pose challenges for individuals with specific preferences or accessibility needs. Furthermore, occasional inconsistencies in the user experience across different devices or browsers may lead to frustration among some users, detracting from an otherwise streamlined shopping journey.
